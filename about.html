About the Archive

This project intends to explore history through the lens of a less visible form of political media. While some attention has been drawn to videogames as a sociological influencer, it is often missed that the scope of this issue goes beyond the mindless violence scare of the 1990s. The concept of persuasion in within videogames is that, being conditioned to a game’s rules, in turn conditions the mind to understand said rules as acceptable, or normalised. This has been documented since the war on terror, with games such as America’s Army (2002), although the history of persuasion in videogames goes much further back.

If an obscure game made at the brink of an imperialist project led to increased conscription in the early 2000s, we may see games of the past have similarly influenced people’s minds.
